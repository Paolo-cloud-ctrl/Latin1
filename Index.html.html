Exercise Set 1: Beginner Level

Exercise 1A: Understanding the Third Declension

The third declension in Latin includes nouns of all three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) with various stem patterns. In medical terminology, many anatomical structures and conditions use third declension nouns.

Key characteristics of third declension nouns:

  • The genitive singular typically ends in -is
  • Nominative singular endings vary (-x, -s, -r, -o, -en, etc.)
  • Neuter nouns have the same form in nominative and accusative cases

Exercise 3E: Advanced Case Usage

1. Medicus morbum cordis diagnosticat.

Case: genitive

Reason for usage: The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship - "disease of the heart"

2. Chirurgus pulmonem examinat.

Case: accusative

Reason for usage: The accusative case is used for the direct object of the verb - "examines the lung"

3. Medicamentum in sanguine dissolvitur.

Case: ablative

Reason for usage: The ablative case with the preposition "in" indicates location - "in the blood"
